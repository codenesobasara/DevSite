If you're running a business and need a website or an app, the temptation to grab a template is real. It's fast, it's cheap, and it looks decent out of the box.

But here's the problem: **templates are built for everyone, which means they're optimized for no one.**

## The Template Trap

When you use a page builder like WordPress, Wix, or Squarespace, you're renting someone else's infrastructure. You're limited by their plugins, their update cycles, and their performance ceilings.

For a personal blog? That's fine. For a business that's trying to grow, generate leads, and compete? It's a bottleneck.

## What Custom Actually Means

Custom doesn't mean expensive. It means **intentional.**

- Every page is designed around your conversion goals
- Every feature exists because your business needs it
- Performance is optimized for your specific use case
- You own the code — no vendor lock-in, no monthly fees for basic functionality

## When to Go Custom

You should consider custom software when:

1. **Your business has unique workflows** that off-the-shelf tools can't handle
2. **You need to integrate multiple systems** (CRM, payments, inventory, etc.)
3. **Performance matters** — slow sites lose customers
4. **You want to own your platform** instead of renting it

## The Bottom Line

Templates get you started. Custom software gets you ahead. If your business is past the "just get something up" phase and you're ready to compete, it's time to build something real.

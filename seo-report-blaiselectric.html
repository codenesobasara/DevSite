Critical Issues

These are actively harming your search rankings and leaving your site vulnerable. Fix immediately.
No HSTS (Strict-Transport-Security) Header
Critical
Ranking Impact: Google downgrades sites vulnerable to man-in-the-middle attacks
None of your 129 pages set the Strict-Transport-Security header. HSTS tells browsers to always connect via HTTPS — without it, visitors can be intercepted on insecure connections before the redirect to HTTPS happens. Google considers this a security vulnerability and ranks sites without HSTS below those that have it. Worse, browsers like Chrome may show a "Not Secure" warning in certain scenarios, immediately destroying visitor trust.
The fix: Add the header Strict-Transport-Security: max-age=31536000; includeSubDomains to all responses. This is configured in your WordPress server (likely through Cloudflare, since that's your CDN) or via a security plugin like Really Simple SSL.
All 129 pages affected
Missing Content Security Policy (CSP)
Critical
Ranking Impact: Sites without CSP are vulnerable to XSS — Google penalizes insecure sites
Your site has no Content-Security-Policy header on any page. CSP prevents cross-site scripting attacks by controlling which scripts and resources can execute on your pages. Without it, a single vulnerability in a WordPress plugin could allow attackers to inject malicious code into your site — which Google would detect and flag, potentially removing you from search results entirely (a "manual action" penalty).
The fix: Implement a CSP header through Cloudflare Transform Rules or a WordPress security plugin. Start with a report-only policy to identify issues, then enforce. Example: Content-Security-Policy: default-src 'self'; script-src 'self' 'unsafe-inline'
All 129 pages affected
81 Pages Missing Meta Descriptions
Critical
Ranking Impact: 63% of your site has no description — devastating for click-through rates
81 out of 129 pages (63%) have completely empty meta descriptions. The meta description is the text snippet shown below your link in Google search results — it's your "sales pitch" to the searcher. When it's blank, Google auto-generates one by pulling random text from your page, which almost never compels a click. Studies show that well-written descriptions improve click-through rates by 5-10%. At 63% of your site missing them, you're losing significant organic traffic to competitors who have them.
The fix: Write unique, compelling meta descriptions (150-160 characters) for every page. Use your SEO plugin (likely Yoast or RankMath) to add them. Prioritize service pages first, then blog categories, then individual posts.
81 pages — mostly category/tag archives and blog pagination
10 Pages Missing H1 Headings
Critical
Ranking Impact: H1 is the strongest on-page signal for what a page is about
10 pages have no H1 heading tag at all. The H1 is the single most important on-page SEO element — it tells Google the primary topic of the page. Without it, Google has to guess from surrounding content, which means lower confidence in ranking you for your target keywords. These include your blog landing page and all paginated blog pages — the pages you'd want ranking for informational queries.
The fix: Add clear, keyword-rich H1 headings to all affected pages. For the blog page, something like "Electrical Tips & Green Energy Insights" — for the resources page, match it to the page's purpose.

  • /general/resources-for-local-guelph-area-clients/
TLSv1.2 Not Supported
Critical
Ranking Impact: Incompatible devices cannot access your site at all
Your server only supports TLSv1.3 with no TLSv1.2 fallback. While TLSv1.3 is the latest standard, many corporate networks, older Android devices, and some crawlers still require TLSv1.2. This means a portion of your potential customers — particularly businesses on older enterprise networks looking for commercial electrical work — may get a connection error instead of your website.
The fix: Enable TLSv1.2 in your Cloudflare SSL/TLS settings. Go to SSL/TLS → Edge Certificates → Minimum TLS Version and set it to TLS 1.2 (not 1.3).

No Brotli Compression
High
Ranking Impact: Directly affects Core Web Vitals page speed scores
128 pages are not served with Brotli compression. Brotli reduces page transfer size by 15-25% compared to gzip, directly improving load times. Since Google uses Core Web Vitals as a ranking factor, uncompressed pages that load slower will rank below compressed competitors. For a 129-page WordPress site with images and scripts, this adds up to significant bandwidth waste.
The fix: Enable Brotli in Cloudflare: Speed → Optimization → Content Optimization → Brotli. Toggle it on. Instant improvement across all pages.
128 out of 129 pages
Missing ARIA Labels (108 Elements)
High
Ranking Impact: Accessibility is a confirmed Google ranking signal
108 interactive elements across your site are missing ARIA labels. These tell Google and screen readers what buttons, links, and form fields do. Google uses accessibility as a ranking factor — sites that score poorly here lose position to accessible competitors. For a service business, this also impacts conversions: users with assistive technology cannot navigate to your contact or quote request pages.
The fix: Add descriptive aria-label attributes to all buttons, nav links, and interactive elements. Check your WordPress theme's template files and any page builder elements (sliders, menus, CTAs).

Duplicate Blog Titles & Descriptions (9 Pages)
Medium
Ranking Impact: Duplicate content signals confuse Google about which page to rank
9 pages share the exact same title "Blog | Blais Electric" and the same meta description. This is your blog's paginated pages (/blog/, /blog/?query-14-page=1, etc.). When Google sees multiple pages with identical titles and descriptions, it doesn't know which one to show in results — so it may show none of them, or pick one at random. The fix: Add unique title suffixes (e.g., "Blog - Page 2 | Blais Electric") and set rel="canonical" on paginated pages pointing back to /blog/. Your SEO plugin should handle this — check its pagination settings.

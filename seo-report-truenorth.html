Critical Issues

These are actively harming your search rankings and must be addressed immediately.
Missing Content Security Policy (CSP)
Critical
Ranking Impact: Google flags insecure sites and may show browser warnings
None of your 17 pages have a Content-Security-Policy header. CSP is a critical security layer that prevents cross-site scripting (XSS) attacks — one of the most common ways websites get hacked. Without it, malicious code could be injected into your pages. Google increasingly factors site security into rankings, and browsers may display security warnings to visitors, driving them away before they ever see your content.
The fix: Implement a Content-Security-Policy header that restricts which scripts, styles, and resources can load on your pages. This needs to be configured at the hosting/platform level.
All 17 pages affected
Platform limitation: Your site is built on Wix. Wix does not currently allow custom security headers like CSP. This fix would require migrating to a platform that gives you server-level control (e.g., Vercel, Netlify, or a custom host).
TLSv1.2 Protocol Not Supported
Critical
Ranking Impact: Google requires HTTPS — incomplete TLS support breaks compatibility
Your server only supports TLSv1.3 and does not offer TLSv1.2 as a fallback. While TLSv1.3 is newer, many corporate networks, older devices, and some search engine crawlers still use TLSv1.2. Without it, these visitors and bots may fail to connect to your site entirely — meaning Google may not be able to crawl certain pages, and a portion of your potential customers literally cannot access your site.
The fix: Enable TLSv1.2 support alongside TLSv1.3. This is configured at the hosting/server level and requires no code changes.
Platform limitation: This is controlled by Wix's infrastructure. You cannot change TLS settings on Wix. Contact Wix support or consider migration.
Blog Page Missing H1 Heading
Critical
Ranking Impact: H1 is the #1 on-page SEO signal for page topic
Your /blog page has no H1 heading tag. The H1 tells Google what the page is about — it's the single most important on-page SEO element. Without it, Google has to guess your page's topic from other signals, which means it will rank lower for your target keywords. Think of it as a book chapter with no title — search engines simply don't know what to file it under.
The fix: Add a clear, keyword-rich H1 heading to the blog page (e.g., "Concrete Forming Tips & Industry News"). This can be done in the Wix editor.
1 page — /blog

High Priority Issues

These issues significantly reduce your ranking potential and should be fixed soon.
Missing Security Headers
High
Ranking Impact: Insecure sites get ranked below secure competitors
Your site is missing several important security headers: X-Frame-Options (prevents clickjacking), Referrer-Policy (controls data leakage), and Permissions-Policy (restricts browser features). Together, these leave your site vulnerable to common attacks. Google's ranking algorithm considers site security — missing protections mean your competitors with proper security will outrank you for the same keywords.
The fix: Add X-Frame-Options: SAMEORIGIN, Referrer-Policy: strict-origin-when-cross-origin, and Permissions-Policy headers to all responses.
All 17 pages missing these headers
Platform limitation: Wix does not expose custom response header configuration. This requires platform migration to resolve fully.
Missing Accessibility Labels (ARIA)
High
Ranking Impact: Accessibility is a confirmed Google ranking signal
17 pages are missing ARIA labels on interactive elements, and 3 pages have critically inaccessible elements. ARIA labels tell search engines and screen readers what buttons, links, and form fields do. Google uses accessibility signals in ranking — sites that score poorly here lose position to accessible competitors. This also means users with disabilities cannot use your site, limiting your audience.
The fix: Add aria-label attributes to all buttons, navigation links, and interactive elements. In Wix, check that all buttons and links have descriptive text or labels set in the accessibility panel.
17 pages with warnings, 3 pages critical
No Brotli Compression
High
Ranking Impact: Page speed is a Core Web Vitals factor
None of your pages are served with Brotli compression. Brotli reduces page size by 15-25% over standard gzip, directly improving load times. Since 2021, Google uses Core Web Vitals (including page speed) as a direct ranking factor. Without compression, your pages transfer more data than necessary — every extra millisecond of load time costs you both rankings and conversions.
The fix: Enable Brotli compression at the server/CDN level.
All 17 pages affected
Platform limitation: Wix controls compression settings. This cannot be changed without platform migration.
Excessive DOM Depth (30+ Levels)
High
Ranking Impact: Deep DOM trees slow rendering and hurt Core Web Vitals
9 of your 17 pages have DOM trees deeper than 30 levels. This means your page's HTML structure is excessively nested — like folders inside folders inside folders. Deep DOM trees make the browser work harder to render the page, slowing down paint times and interaction responsiveness. Google measures this through Interaction to Next Paint (INP), and deeply nested pages score poorly.
The fix: Flatten the HTML structure by reducing unnecessary wrapper elements. On Wix, this is largely controlled by the platform's output — simplifying your layout sections and reducing nested containers helps.
9 out of 17 pages

Medium Priority Recommendations

These won't tank your rankings alone, but fixing them gives you an edge over competitors.
Missing Image Alt Text
Medium
Ranking Impact: Alt text is how Google "sees" your images
5 pages have images without alt attributes. Alt text describes what an image shows — without it, Google cannot index your images for Google Image Search (a significant traffic source for construction companies), and visually impaired visitors have no idea what's in the image. Every image without alt text is a missed keyword opportunity.
The fix: Add descriptive alt text to every image (e.g., "Residential concrete foundation pour in Kitchener Ontario" instead of "IMG_2847"). Do this in the Wix media manager.
5 pages
Skipped Heading Levels
Medium
Ranking Impact: Broken hierarchy confuses Google's content parsing
3 pages jump from H1 directly to H3, or start with H2 instead of H1. Heading hierarchy (H1 → H2 → H3) creates a content outline that Google reads to understand your page structure. Skipping levels is like writing a book outline that jumps from Chapter 1 to Section 1.3 — it confuses the reader about what's important and what's a subtopic.
The fix: Restructure headings so they follow proper order. The /blog page needs an H1, and footer sections should use H4/H5 or styled paragraphs instead of H3 tags.
3 pages — /blog, /privacy-policy, +1
Images Not Served in Modern Formats
Medium
Ranking Impact: Affects Largest Contentful Paint (LCP) score
No WebP or AVIF images detected. These modern formats deliver the same visual quality at 25-50% smaller file sizes than JPEG/PNG. Google's Largest Contentful Paint metric — one of three Core Web Vitals — measures how fast your biggest visual element loads. For a construction company site with project photos, this can be a major speed improvement.
The fix: Convert images to WebP format before uploading. Wix has some automatic format conversion built in, but verify it's active in your site settings under "Performance."
Duplicate Empty Meta Descriptions
Medium
Ranking Impact: Missing descriptions reduce click-through rates from search results
2 pages have empty meta descriptions (11% of your site). The meta description is the text snippet shown below your link in Google search results. When it's blank, Google auto-generates one — often pulling random text from your page that doesn't compel clicks. Pages with well-written descriptions get 5-10% more clicks than those without.
The fix: Write unique, compelling descriptions (150-160 characters) for every page. Include your target keyword and a call to action.
2 pages with empty descriptions
Missing Form Labels
Medium
Ranking Impact: Poor form accessibility reduces conversion and SEO score
5 pages have form inputs without proper labels. This makes forms difficult for screen readers and auto-fill tools to understand. For a business that relies on form submissions (quotes, contact requests), inaccessible forms directly reduce your conversion rate — visitors give up if the form doesn't work smoothly on their device.
The fix: Ensure all form fields have associated <label> elements. In Wix, check that every input field in your forms has a visible label or placeholder configured.

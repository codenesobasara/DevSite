High Priority Issues

These issues are actively reducing your search rankings and should be addressed first.
Missing Accessibility Labels (ARIA)
High
Ranking Impact: Accessibility is a confirmed Google ranking signal
185 interactive elements on your site (buttons, links, form fields) are missing ARIA labels. These labels tell search engines and screen readers what each element does. Google uses accessibility as a ranking factor — sites that score poorly here get ranked lower than accessible competitors. This also means visually impaired users cannot navigate your site, which limits your potential audience.
The fix: Add descriptive aria-label attributes to all interactive elements. This involves updating your component templates to include labels like "Navigate to Kitchener market report" instead of unlabelled links.
185 elements across 75 pages
No Brotli Compression Enabled
High
Ranking Impact: Page speed is a Core Web Vitals factor
None of your pages are served with Brotli compression — a modern compression algorithm that reduces page size by 15-25% over standard gzip. Google's Core Web Vitals directly measure how fast your pages load. Without compression, your pages transfer more data than necessary, increasing load times on mobile and slower connections. Since 2021, Google uses page speed as a direct ranking factor.
The fix: Enable Brotli compression at the hosting/CDN level. Your site runs on Vercel, which supports Brotli — it may need to be explicitly enabled in your configuration.
All 75 pages affected
Missing ARIA Roles on Key Elements
High
Ranking Impact: Helps Google understand your page structure
8 structural elements are missing role attributes (navigation, main, banner, etc.). These roles create a semantic map of your page that Google uses to understand content hierarchy. Without them, search engines have to guess which part of your page is the main content vs. navigation vs. footer — reducing their confidence in ranking your content for relevant queries.
The fix: Add role="navigation", role="main", role="banner", and role="contentinfo" to the appropriate landmark elements in your layout.

Medium Priority Recommendations

These won't tank your rankings alone, but fixing them gives you an edge over competitors.
Images Not Served in Modern Formats
Medium
Ranking Impact: Affects Largest Contentful Paint (LCP) score
Your images are served as traditional JPEG/PNG. Modern formats like WebP and AVIF deliver the same visual quality at 25-50% smaller file sizes. Google's Largest Contentful Paint metric — one of three Core Web Vitals �� directly measures how fast your biggest visual element loads. Smaller images = faster LCP = better ranking position.
The fix: Convert images to WebP format and serve them with proper fallbacks. If using Next.js Image component, this may already be partially handled — needs verification.
47 Unnecessary Redirects
Medium
Ranking Impact: Wastes crawl budget and adds latency
Every page on basara.ca (non-www) redirects to www.basara.ca via a 307 redirect. This means Google has to follow two hops to reach your content — wasting "crawl budget" (the number of pages Google will crawl in a session). It also adds ~30-50ms of latency per page for real visitors. For a 75-page site this is manageable, but it's unnecessary overhead.
The fix: Ensure all internal links point directly to www.basara.ca. Update canonical tags, sitemap, and any external profiles to use the www version.
Server Technology Disclosed
Medium
Ranking Impact: None directly — this is a security best practice
Your server responds with a "Server: Vercel" header on every request. While this doesn't affect SEO directly, it tells potential attackers exactly what platform you're on, making it easier to target known vulnerabilities. It's a quick hardening step.
The fix: Add a custom header configuration to suppress or override the Server header in your Vercel deployment settings.
